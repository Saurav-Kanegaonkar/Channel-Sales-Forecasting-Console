# Executive Findings

## Recommendation

Use the dealer priority queue as the monthly sales leadership readout. It identifies where forecast miss, incentive exposure, promotion ROI, territory coverage, and data quality overlap.

## Key findings

- The highest-priority partners are not always the highest-volume partners. They are the partners where multiple risk signals compound.
- Forecast variance should be reviewed alongside inventory coverage. A forecast miss is more urgent when demand is rising and coverage is thin.
- Open incentive exposure needs to be visible before payout close, especially when a partner also has forecast variance or promotion risk.
- Territory coverage pressure creates a practical constraint on which recommendations can be executed this month.
- Source-system exceptions should be shown next to recommendations so sales leaders know which numbers are ready for action.

## Operating cadence

- Monday: refresh dealer sales, forecast, promotion, compensation, and source-event files.
- Tuesday: review priority queue with regional managers.
- Wednesday: lock forecast corrections and incentive review list.
- Thursday: decide which promotions to continue, pause, or resize.
- Friday: publish executive readout with action owners.
